2nd Balkan war

Information


Conflict:


    Bulgaria was dissatisfied with the first Balkan war, so they attacked Greece and Serbia (their former allies). Romania and the and the Ottoman Empire joined against Bulgaria.



    Resolution:


    The Treaty of Bucharest ended the War. Bulgaria suffered significant territorial losses, and the borders of the Balkan states were further adjusted.







    History - After War



    Bulgaria:


    - After territorial losses in the Second Balkan War and political instability, Bulgaria underwent a period of recovery. By 1928, it stabilized but it still had to deal with the consequences of the war.




    Greece:


    - Greece got territorial gains and increased influence in the region. Greece faced political changes and social developments. It continued to be a key player in the Balkans.




    Serbia:


    - Serbia was benefited with the territorial changes following the Balkan Wars. By 1928, it had evolved into the Kingdom of Yugoslavia after the end of World War 1. It was an important state in the Balkans, although facing internal challenges.




    Romania:


    - Romania had expanded its territory after the Second Balkan War and further during World War I. By 1928, Romania was undergoing political changes and grappling with the aftermath of WWorld War 1, including the establishment of a Greater Romania.




    Ottoman Empire/Turkey:


    - The Ottoman Empire had lost most of its European territories in the Balkan Wars. By 1928, the Ottoman Empire had transitioned into the Republic of Turkey under the leadership of Mustafa Kemal Atatürk. Turkey was undergoing a process of modernization and secularization.
