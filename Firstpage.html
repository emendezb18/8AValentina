2nd Balkan war

Information

Political Boundaries:


    - The combined forces of Serbia, Greece and Romania defeated Bulgaria.


    - The second Balkan war broke out based on the first Balkan war’s result.


    - The second war’s participants were: Serbia, Greece, Romania, Bulgaria, Macedonia, Montenegro and Turkey


    - Serbia and Greece made an alliance against Bulgaria.


    - Political boundaries and territory change are showed in the maps below.


    - After the war, Bulgaria enlarged its territory about 16%, Serbia almost doubled its size, Greece expanded about 68% and Bulgaria lost Adrianople and eastern thrace.

Ottoman Empire:


- In the Ottoman Empire people practiced Islam, but now it isn’t practiced as much, now people are mostly Christians, Jews and other religious minorities.


- IPeople in the Ottoman Empire mostly speak Ottoman Turkish, Arabic and Persian.



Bulgaria:


- In Bulgaria 85% of the population speaks Bulgarian and 2.5% speak Macedonian.


- In Bulgaria people practice Christianity and some Islam.



Romania:


- Romania’s main religion is Christianity.


- People speak Romanian, Italian, Hungarian and Romani.



Montenegro:


- People in Montenegro mainly speak Montenegrin, but they also speak.


- Serbian, Croatian, Bosnian and Albanian.


- Almost all of Montenegro’s population practice Eastern Orthodox Christianity.



Serbia:


- People in Serbia speak Serbian, Bosnian, Croatian and some Hungaria.


- In Serbia, 84% of the population identifies as Eastern Orthodox, 5% as Catholic, 3% as Muslim and 1% as Protestant.



Greece:


- In Greece, people speak mostly Greek, but they also speak English, German, French and Italian.


- 90% of the population in Greece practice Christian Orthodox, and the rest of the populations is Muslim, Catholic, Jewish and other minorities.
